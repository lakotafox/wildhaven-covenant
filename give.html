How this becomes tax-deductible

We're not hiding the ball. There are exactly two honest routes, and founding contributions pay to walk them:

Route A — Fiscal sponsor (fast, legitimate)

An existing 501(c)(3) "fiscal sponsor" agrees to receive tax-deductible gifts on Wildhaven's behalf for a small fee while Wildhaven operates as a project under them. This is a well-established, fully legal model — donors get deductibility now, through the sponsor.

Route B — Incorporate + IRS 1023

Incorporate as a nonprofit, get an EIN, adopt bylaws/board, file IRS Form 1023 for 501(c)(3) status, and register for charitable solicitation in each state we fundraise in. Once the IRS determination letter is issued, gifts are deductible directly.

When either route is complete, this page will say so plainly and the wording will change to "tax-deductible donation" — not one day before. Until then: founding contribution, full stop.

FAQ

Is my contribution tax-deductible?

No — not yet. Wildhaven is not yet a 501(c)(3). It's a founding contribution toward forming the organization. See the two routes above for how/when that changes.

Where does the money go?

Incorporation and filing fees, the 1023 / fiscal-sponsor process, state charitable-solicitation registration, and the first stewardship endowment. The unglamorous spine that makes "forever" real.

Is this a church or a tax dodge?

No. It is intended to be a secular nonprofit. The earth-reverent identity is cultural and lives in the Covenant — never used as a tax shield. See Article V.

Can I give monthly?

Recurring giving turns on once the organization is formed and the dedicated nonprofit account is live. For now: a one-time founding contribution, or email us to be contacted when recurring opens.
