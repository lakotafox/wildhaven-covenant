Donate land — or an easement on land you love

The single highest-impact thing most people can do. Two ways:

Outright land gift

Give us the parcel. We hold it forever, fund its endowment, and you may receive a charitable deduction for its appraised value. Your land is removed from the market permanently.

Conservation easement

Keep your land, keep living on it — permanently give up the right to develop or subdivide it. We hold and defend that restriction forever, including against future owners.

Land and easement gifts have real tax and legal consequences and require independent appraisal and counsel. We'll walk it through with you; we don't give you tax advice.

FAQ

Is this a church? Is it a tax dodge?

No. Wildhaven is described as a secular 501(c)(3) public charity (conservation + prevention of cruelty to animals). The earth-reverent identity is real and public, but it is never used as a tax shield. Reverence in the open; boring structure underneath. See Article V of the Covenant.

What does "forever" actually mean?

Deed restrictions, perpetual conservation easements, a charter ban on selling protected land, and a funded stewardship endowment per parcel for monitoring and legal defense after the founders are gone.

Why pair land with animals?

Because protected land needs defenders and rescued animals need protected land. Splitting them weakens both. See Article III.

Is my gift tax-deductible?

Cash and land gifts to a qualified 501(c)(3) are generally deductible to the extent allowed by law; land/easement gifts require independent appraisal and counsel. We are in formation — confirm specifics with your own advisor.
