We hold wild land in trust. Forever.

Wildhaven Covenant is a stewardship land trust and wildlife sanctuary. We acquire and permanently protect wild land — anywhere — and let rescued and recovering animals heal on the very land they help keep wild. Land protection and animal care, one mission, one promise: forever.

Our Mission

Most conservation money rents impact. We buy permanence. Every dollar that comes through Wildhaven Covenant goes toward land that is protected in perpetuity by enforceable legal instruments — not a 5-year grant, not a campaign, but a promise that outlives every person who made it.

The land doesn't sit empty. Our protected preserves double as release sites and lifelong sanctuary for wildlife that can't be returned to the wild. The animals get a home; the habitat gets defenders. Neither works alone. That's the whole idea.

The Stewardship Covenant (who we are)

Wildhaven is, on paper, a boring and bulletproof secular nonprofit. But it has a soul, and we don't hide it. Everyone who joins signs the Stewardship Covenant — our plain-language creed about what we owe the living world. An excerpt:

"We did not make the wild and we will not own it. We are not its masters but its stewards, and a steward's only power is restraint. We promise the land what almost no one promises it: not improvement, not use — permanence. We hold it so that what lives there may go on living, long after our names are forgotten."

Fee-Simple Ownership

The trust buys or receives the land and owns it outright. Best for sanctuary and release sites — we control the habitat directly and can host recovering animals. The land never re-enters the market because the trust's charter forbids selling protected parcels.

Conservation Easement

A landowner keeps and lives on their land but permanently gives up the right to develop or subdivide it. We hold that restriction and are legally bound to monitor and defend it in perpetuity — including against future owners. Lower cost, enormous reach.

The Stewardship Endowment

The part rookie land trusts get fatally wrong: protection costs money forever. Every parcel we accept arrives with a funded endowment for perpetual monitoring and legal defense. No endowment, no acceptance. This is why "forever" is credible here.
