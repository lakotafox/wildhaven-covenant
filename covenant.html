The Stewardship Covenant

This is the document everyone who joins Wildhaven signs. It is our identity. It is sincere. It is also not our tax structure — Wildhaven is a secular 501(c)(3). The reverence is real and lives here, in the open; the legal machinery underneath it is deliberately boring.

Preamble

We did not make the wild and we will not own it. We are not its masters but its stewards, and a steward's only true power is restraint. We promise the land what almost no one promises it: not improvement, not use — permanence. We hold it so that what lives there may go on living, long after our names are forgotten.

The Seven Articles

I.  Permanence over use

We acquire land to remove it from the market, not to develop, extract, or flip it. Protected land at Wildhaven is never sold. "Forever" is written into our charter, our easements, and our deeds — not just our hearts.

II.  Restraint over control

The wild does not need our management so much as our absence. We intervene only to undo harm, restore what was broken, or care for an animal that can no longer care for itself — and then we step back.

III.  The animal and the land are one promise

We do not choose between protecting habitat and rescuing creatures. Rescued wildlife heals on protected ground; protected ground gains creatures who make it whole. To split them is to misunderstand both.

IV.  Fund the forever

A promise we cannot keep is a lie we tell the land. Every parcel we accept arrives with a funded stewardship endowment for perpetual monitoring and legal defense. We would rather protect one acre truly than a thousand acres in name.

V.  Reverence in the open, structure underneath

Our awe of the living world is sincere and we will not hide it. But our legal form is a plain secular charity. We will never use belief as a shield from accountability, taxes, or scrutiny. The soul is public; the books are too.

VI.  No private gain

No steward, officer, or founder profits from this land beyond reasonable, documented pay for honest work. Money given for the mission is spent on the mission. This is both a moral line and the law, and we treat them as the same line.

VII.  Outlive yourself

The point of stewardship is that it does not end with the steward. We build governance, endowments, and easements designed to keep the promise after every founding voice is gone. If it depends on us personally, we have already failed.

What this means in plain terms

The Covenant says… In practice that is…
"Permanence over use" Deed restrictions + charter ban on selling protected land
"Fund the forever" No parcel accepted without a stewardship endowment
"Reverence in the open" Secular 501(c)(3); no "church" tax claim, ever
"No private gain" Independent board, published finances, reasonable-comp policy
The Stewardship Covenant is a statement of values and culture. It is not a contract, and it is not legal or tax advice. Wildhaven Covenant is described throughout as a secular charitable organization in formation.
